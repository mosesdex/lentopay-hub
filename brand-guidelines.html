Brand Foundation

Lentopay is a Nigeria-first crypto exchange and everyday-money app: buy, sell and swap crypto, pay bills with crypto, trade gift cards, save, and invest with E-Fund. Everything we make should feel like money moving - quickly, safely, without friction.

Mission

Make moving money in Nigeria - naira, crypto, or anything in between - feel effortless, transparent and trustworthy.

The Brand Story

Our monogram is an L and a P drawn with one continuous stroke. The L doesn't end - it flows, turns, and becomes the P. That single unbroken line is the whole idea of Lentopay: connection, integration, and the seamless movement of money between two parties. A sender and a receiver. A buyer and a merchant. Naira and USDT. One flow, no seams - payments that actually work.

Logo System

The identity is a Royal Blue badge carrying the LP monogram, paired with a lowercase Sora Bold wordmark. Use the assets exactly as constructed here - never redraw, restyle or retype any part.

Primary lockup

Badge + wordmark, baseline-aligned, with a gap of 0.375× badge width. Descriptors ("exchange", "gateway") are set in DM Sans 400 at reduced weight beside the wordmark.

lentopay
Full color on light - default
lentopay
Inverted on Night - white badge, Royal glyphs
lentopay exchange
Product descriptor lockup - DM Sans 400, Fog-toned
Monogram only - app icons, avatars, favicons, tight spaces

Use the monogram alone wherever the wordmark would render below legible size, or where the brand is already established in context (app icon, in-app header, social avatar, watermarks). Everywhere else, prefer the full lockup.

Construction & clear space

The unit x equals one quarter of the badge height (the monogram's stroke zone). Keep a minimum clear space of 1x on all sides of the lockup - no text, graphics or competing marks inside it. Badge corner radius is fixed at 25% of badge height.

Minimum sizes

24 px
Minimum badge size, digital. Below this, glyph strokes lose definition.
8 mm
Minimum badge size, print. Verify strokes hold on uncoated stock.

Contrast minimums

4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text. Never rely on color alone - pair every state color with an icon or a text label.

Gold is not for body text

Saffron Gold must never be used for body text on white or Off White backgrounds. It is an accent: highlights, markers, small graphic moments.

Interactive contrast

Buttons, links, inputs and focus indicators must maintain at least 4.5:1 contrast against their surroundings in every state.

Iconography & Illustration

One icon family, drawn in-house on a 24px grid: 1.75px strokes, rounded caps and joins, geometry that echoes the monogram's continuous flow. Icons are always inline SVG.

Do
  • Draw on the 24px grid, 1.75px stroke, rounded caps and joins.
  • Use currentColor so icons inherit text color and states.
  • Keep 2px optical padding inside the viewBox; align strokes to the half-pixel.
  • Illustrations: flat Fog/Royal shapes, one Gold accent, generous negative space.
Don't
  • No emoji as icons - anywhere, ever. Product, marketing or support.
  • No mixed icon families (filled + outlined, or different stroke weights) in one surface.
  • No raster icons or icon fonts - inline SVG only.
  • No 3D, gradients or shadows inside icons.

Voice & Tone

The Lentopay voice is one formula, held in tension: confident but never boastful, warm but never chummy, direct but never rude.

Confident

never boastful
TimidBoastful

We state facts about rails, settlement and uptime plainly. We don't call ourselves revolutionary - we show numbers.

Warm

never chummy
ColdChummy

Human and patient, especially when money is stuck. No slang overload, no fake familiarity, no "hey bestie".

Direct

never rude
VagueBlunt

Plain verbs, short sentences. Lead with what happened, then what happens next, then when.

Rewrites - officialese out, Lentopay in

Not thisThis
"Kindly endeavour to ensure your KYC documentation is duly submitted prior to transacting."
"Add your ID to start trading. It takes about 2 minutes."
"Our cutting-edge, synergistic payment infrastructure leverages best-in-class rails."
"One API. Every payment method in Nigeria."
"Your transaction could not be processed at this time. Please try again later."
"This transfer failed - your ₦25,000 was not debited. Try again now, or pay by USSD instead."
"The platform will be undergoing scheduled maintenance activities during the aforementioned window."
"We're down for maintenance Sunday, 2:00-2:30 AM WAT. Queued transfers complete automatically after."

Microcopy rules

  • Say what happens next - and when. "Your transaction is pending. We'll update you in 5 minutes." Every waiting state gets a time expectation we can actually meet.
  • Honest error states. Say what failed, whether money moved, and the user's best next step. Never hide a failure behind "something went wrong".
  • Verbs first, plain and short. "Send", "Swap", "Withdraw" - not "Initiate disbursement". One idea per sentence.
  • Numbers over adjectives. "Settles in under 10 seconds" beats "lightning-fast". If we can't guarantee the number, we don't print it.
07

Motion Principles

Motion at Lentopay is functional: it confirms, orients and reassures. Fast enough to feel instant, slow enough to be read.

150-300ms

Micro-interactions

Button presses, toggles, chips, hovers. Anything the finger touches responds inside 300ms.

≤400ms

Transitions

Screen changes, sheets, modals. Never make the user wait for choreography.

ease-out / ease-in

Enter fast, exit faster

Entrances decelerate (cubic-bezier(0, 0, 0.2, 1)); exits accelerate (cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)).

Movement always means money moving.

Motion travels left → right or bottom → top - like a transaction completing, a balance rising. Nothing drifts aimlessly; nothing bounces for decoration.

Respect prefers-reduced-motion

When the OS requests reduced motion, all movement collapses to simple opacity fades of 200ms or less - including the logo animations. This is implemented in code, not just recommended.

Nigerian Market Guidelines

Nigeria-first is a design constraint, not a marketing line. Rails, trust signals and language register are specified - not left to taste.

Payment channel hierarchy

Order every payment-method list, checkout and onboarding flow this way:

  1. Bank transferDefault rail - cheapest, most trusted
  2. CardsFamiliar, but higher failure rates
  3. USSDWorks on any phone, no data needed
  4. Mobile moneyGrowing fast; regional strongholds
  5. Crypto railsPower users; always show ₦ equivalent

Trust signals checklist

  • CBN and SEC licensing references present in every footer and app "About" screen.
  • PCI-DSS and ISO 27001 badges rendered in brand style (stroke icons, not third-party bitmaps).
  • Publish uptime and median settlement-time stats - real numbers, updated, dated.
  • Never promise settlement times we cannot guarantee. "Usually under 10 seconds" only if measured.
  • SMS-friendly, not-intimidating tone; data-light patterns for low-bandwidth users.

Language register

Not thisThis
"Kindly be informed that your request is receiving due attention."
"We're on it. You'll hear from us within 24 hours."
"Revert back to us with the aforementioned documentation at your earliest convenience."
"Send us your ID when you're ready - reply to this message."
Over-familiar slang in transactional messages ("Omo! Your cash don land!")
"₦50,000 received from Tunde A. Your new balance is ₦132,500." Warm belongs in marketing; precise belongs in money messages.

₦ formatting rules

₦1,254,300.50

Symbol before the amount, no space. Thousands separators always. Kobo shown as two decimals when present.

₦82,500 ≈ 50.00 USDT

Dual display where crypto is involved: ₦ leads, USDT equivalent follows in a dimmed tone.

₦ 9,999.00
₦ 10,001.50

Tabular numerals in every list and table so amounts align. Sora 700 for amounts, always.

−₦45,000.00

Debits use a true minus sign and keep Night color. Alert Red means failure - never merely "money out".
