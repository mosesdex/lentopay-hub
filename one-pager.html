Lentopay Exchange - Executive One-Pager

July 2026 · Confidential · Prepared for Oshodi-Tapa Agboola

Lentopay Exchange is the SEC-licensed, Nigeria-first crypto app that combines global-exchange pricing with instant naira rails and everyday utility - trading, bill payments, gift cards and savings in one account.

The problem

  • ~90% of Nigeria's crypto volume sits on unlicensed rails (Binance, Bybit, informal P2P) - no naira ramp since March 2024, no local recourse, real legal risk.
  • The #1 user complaint market-wide is naira withdrawals that don't arrive; the #1 trust deficit is custody, after FTX, Patricia and the 200k-victim CBEX Ponzi collapse.
  • No licensed player unifies crypto + bills + gift cards + savings - exchanges skip daily utility; gift-card apps skip licensed crypto; Yellow Card exited retail entirely (Jan 2026).

The solution

  • Licensed core: buy/sell/swap at 0.1%, instant naira deposits/withdrawals via SEC-VASP bank rails - with a public SLA ("95% of withdrawals < 5 minutes") on a live status page.
  • Daily utility loop: airtime, data, electricity and TV paid from crypto balances, with 1-3% cashback funded from the VTU wholesale margin - the proven PalmPay-style daily-open driver.
  • Gift card trading (a $2B/yr Nigerian market) inside a licensed wallet with transparent rates, hardened KYC and instant settlement.
  • Trust engineered in: monthly third-party proof-of-reserves from day one (only Luno does this in Africa), regulated custody, live spread shown on every quote.

The market

  • $92.1B - on-chain value received by Nigeria, Jul 2024-Jun 2025 (Chainalysis); #2 globally on grassroots adoption.
  • ~22M - Nigerian crypto users (~10% of population); 81.5% aged 18-34; stablecoins ~43% of regional volume.
  • ~$20B/yr - remittance flows where stablecoins beat 8%+ traditional fees; naira lost ~41% in 2024 alone, driving digital-dollar demand.

Business model

0.1% maker/taker · 0.5-1% swap spread (displayed live) · ₦100/₦200 flat fiat fees · 2-4% gift card margin · 1.5-3% VTU margin (~1% back as cashback) · 2-4pp savings NIM at 6-8% APY (Phase 2+). Year-1 base case ~$0.4M revenue on 150k registered users; ~$3-7M by Year 3.

Moat & differentiation

  • Only licensed player bundling trading + bills + gift cards + savings - the whitespace is structural, and the SEC licence (₦2B capital, 9-18 months) is the barrier to copying it.
  • Trust assets competitors don't run: public off-ramp SLA + status page, monthly proof-of-reserves, radical fee/spread disclosure, 48h frozen-funds guarantee.
  • Engagement economics: cashback funded from bills margin (not subsidy), activation-gated referrals - built against the 3-in-5 dormancy failure mode of Nigerian fintech.

Regulatory status & plan

Dual-track to market: direct SEC ARIP filing (₦2B capital committed; target Approval-in-Principle in 9-18 months) in parallel with partnership/acquisition talks with existing AIP holders for a 6-9-month fast path. Compliance built-in from day one: CBN-compliant bank rails, FIRS monthly reporting, NDPC registration, NFIU/AML programme, BVN+NIN+selfie KYC.

Roadmap

  • Phase 0 (Q3-Q4 2026): incorporation, ARIP filing, bank rails, custody + KYC stack; closed beta with live status page.
  • Phase 1 (launch): buy/sell/swap, instant naira ramps, bills with cashback; campus ambassadors + gated referrals.
  • Phase 2: gift card marketplace at scale; savings (6-8% APY) after full VASP registration and SEC product approval.
  • Phase 3: E-Fund tokenized/unlisted-asset investing via a licensed DAOP/crowdfunding partner; regional expansion assessment.

The ask / next steps

Approve ₦2.1-2.5B (~$1.5-1.8M) regulatory capital and the 90-day plan: incorporate and file ARIP, open bank-rail and AIP-partner negotiations, lock launch scope, and appoint the proof-of-reserves attestation firm. Decision review at Day 90: direct licence vs. partner fast path.
